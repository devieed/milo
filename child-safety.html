This page is Milo’s published standards against child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) and child sexual abuse material (CSAM), as required by Google Play’s Child Safety Standards policy for social apps. It applies to the Milo mobile application and related online services.

1. Zero tolerance

Milo has zero tolerance for CSAE, CSAM, grooming, sexualization of minors, trafficking or exploitation of children, and any content or conduct that endangers children.

We prohibit users from:

  • Creating, uploading, sending, soliciting, or sharing CSAM or sexual content involving minors;
  • Grooming, enticement, or sexual exploitation of anyone under 18;
  • Impersonating a minor or misrepresenting age to interact with minors;
  • Sharing personal contact information of minors for harmful purposes;
  • Using Milo to facilitate offline harm to children;
  • Any other conduct that violates child safety laws or this policy.

Violations may result in immediate content removal, account suspension or permanent ban, preservation of evidence as allowed by law, and reporting to authorities.

2. Age requirement

Milo is intended for users aged 16 and older only. We do not knowingly permit children under 16 to create accounts or use the Service. See also our Privacy Policy — Children and Terms of Service.

If we learn that a user is under 16, we will take steps to disable the account and delete associated personal data as soon as reasonably practicable.

3. Prevention and moderation

Milo is a text-focused social letter-writing app. To reduce harm we:

  • Run automated content moderation (sensitive-word checks and third-party text moderation) on letters and replies before or when they are sent;
  • Apply rate limits and abuse controls on accounts, IPs, and messaging;
  • Require email verification for account access;
  • Limit exposure of real profile information until users mutually agree to become pen pals;
  • Review reports and enforcement queues under these standards.

No system is perfect. Users must still report concerns promptly (see Section 5).

4. CSAM response

When we obtain actual knowledge of CSAM or CSAE on Milo, we will:

  1. Remove the content and restrict access as quickly as possible;
  2. Suspend or terminate the responsible account(s);
  3. Preserve relevant records as required for investigation and legal obligations;
  4. Report confirmed CSAM to appropriate authorities, including where applicable the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline (U.S.) or equivalent regional hotlines and law enforcement;
  5. Cooperate with valid legal requests from authorities and with Google Play safety notifications.

We do not allow CSAM on our platform for any reason, including “research,” “awareness,” or “journalism,” unless we are legally required to retain material solely for mandatory reporting—and even then only under strict internal controls.

5. How to report concerns

If you see content or behavior on Milo that may involve CSAE, CSAM, grooming, or a child at risk, report it immediately.

Report to Milo (in-app or email)
Email: support@z-rex.net
Subject line: Child safety report - Milo
Include: description of the concern, relevant usernames or message IDs, dates/times, and screenshots if safe to provide.

From the Milo app: open Profile → Report Safety Concern to email us or view these standards.

If someone is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services first.

Additional resources (examples):

  • U.S.: NCMEC CyberTipline — report.cybertip.org
  • UK: CEOP — ceop.police.uk
  • EU: national hotlines via INHOPE

6. Legal compliance

Z-Rex Network complies with applicable child safety laws and regulations in the regions where we operate, including obligations to report confirmed CSAM to relevant authorities. We update our practices as laws and platform policies evolve.

7. Child safety point of contact

Child safety point of contact (Milo / Z-Rex Network)
Email: support@z-rex.net
Role: Receives Google Play and user reports regarding CSAE/CSAM, coordinates content removal, account enforcement, and mandatory reporting.
Response time: We aim to acknowledge child safety reports within 2 business days and prioritize urgent CSAM reports for same-day review where possible.

8. Updates

We may update these standards. The current version is always available at this URL with an updated effective date. Material changes may also be referenced in our Terms of Service.
